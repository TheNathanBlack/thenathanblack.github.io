Look, I am really well suited for this position, but a traditional resume wouldn't show much of the actual picture of why I would be awesome at this job. So I'm going to try this weirdo approach, which is a better distillation of who I am anyway.

I've been in games my entire working career in one form or another. In the early nineties I managed a board, puzzle, and roleplaying game store. In the early 2000s I started working in the video game industry on titles like Deus Ex, Deus Ex: Invisible War, Thief 3, Trivial Pursuit: Millennium Edition and Barbie Pet Rescue. In recent years I've taken up with the local indie video game scene, working with independent game developers working on passion projects like Spider: Rite of The Shrouded Moon and Monstrocards.

I've personally backed 128 projects on kickstarter and I support over 20 creators on Patreon. I've even been in a kickstarter video for the Monstrocards game. I really believe in crowdfunding and the Kickstarter model as a way forward for interesting new games that might not have had a way to get funded or even noticed in the past. I remember the rush of backing my first project and feeling like I was contributing directly to the good things I wanted to see in the world. Giving directly to the people that do the work is a powerful feeling.

I'm also a part time stunt man, but even that ends up being game related (I play Mario!):

"Nathan Black defines jack-of-all-trades - someone with vast life experience and an easy charisma that combine with deep experience in both digital and tabletop gaming to make him ideally suited for a creator-facing job at Kickstarter."

Jason Morningstar, Bully Pulpit Games, Fiasco, Night Witches

Conventioneer

In more recent years, I've worked with the indie video game crews in Austin, not only on the games themselves, but also working with the local indie game development scene as a whole. I'm down with the Juegos Rancheros crew and I'm helping MC Fantastic Arcade. I'm known locally as a connector, someone who can either get things done or knows the person who can.

I worked at GenCon demoing games for Last Unicorn Games and Cheapass Games back in the early 2000s, more recently I worked show floors doing security for celebrities and managing large crowds for Wizard World Comic Cons in Philadelphia, New Orleans, Portland, and Austin.

I am currently working with Big Bad Con serving as their Conflict Mediator and working on their Community Standards

Project Manager

One of my many career paths is managing projects and small engineering teams. I've lead teams making apps, integrated development environments, and launched games. What I enjoy most about this work is supporting the people on my teams, helping them grow, and removing obstacles from their paths. My basic operating theory is to give engineers the support they need, a clear and reasonable work load and to remove all impediments.
